CLUSTER A — Bitcoin & Macroeconomic Factors (11 papers)
================================================================================

A1.  Karau, S. (2023). Monetary policy and Bitcoin.
     Journal of International Money and Finance, 137(C).
     DOI: 10.1016/j.jimonfin.2023.102880
     URL: https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0261560623000815
     Note: Bitcoin's sensitivity to Fed shocks increased substantially post-2020
           — core empirical precedent for H1.

A2.  Ma, C., Tian, Y., Hsiao, S., & Deng, L. (2022). Monetary policy shocks
     and Bitcoin prices. Research in International Business and Finance, 62(C).
     DOI: 10.1016/j.ribaf.2022.101711
     URL: https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S027553192200099X
     Note: 1bp surprise tightening → -0.25% BTC on FOMC days; benchmark for
           comparing your dFedRate coefficient magnitude.

A3.  Benigno, G., & Rosa, C. (2023). The Bitcoin-macro disconnect.
     Federal Reserve Bank of New York Staff Report No. 1052. SSRN: 4373434.
     URL: https://www.newyorkfed.org/research/staff_reports/sr1052
     Note: Establishes pre-2020 baseline of BTC being orthogonal to macro news
           — the "before" state that your PostETF dummy tests against.

A4.  Che, N., Copestake, A., Furceri, D., & Terracciano, T. (2023). The crypto
     cycle and US monetary policy. IMF Working Paper 2023/163.
     DOI: 10.5089/9798400245411.001
     URL: https://www.imf.org/en/Publications/WP/Issues/2023/08/04/The-Crypto-Cycle-and-US-Monetary-Policy-534834
     Note: Fed tightening → -0.15 SD crypto factor (larger than equities);
           links this explicitly to institutional investor entry. Also relevant
           to Cluster E (balance sheet channel).

A5.  Lin, M., Liu, Y., & Sheng, V. N. K. (2025). Analysis of the impact of
     macroeconomic factors on cryptocurrency returns: Based on a quantile
     regression study. International Review of Economics & Finance, 97(C).
     DOI: 10.1016/j.iref.2024.103757
     URL: https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S1059056024007494
     Note: USD exchange rate and Treasury yields significantly drive BTC returns
           on 2018-2024 data — same macro variables as your model.

A6.  Lyócsa, S., Molnár, P., Plíhal, T., & Širaňová, M. (2020). Impact of
     macroeconomic news, regulation and hacking exchange markets on the
     volatility of Bitcoin. Journal of Economic Dynamics and Control, 119(C).
     DOI: 10.1016/j.jedc.2020.103980
     URL: https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0165188920301482
     Note: Pre-2020: scheduled US macro news had minimal effect on BTC
           volatility — provides the pre-institutional contrast for H1.

A7.  Elsayed, A. H., & Sousa, R. M. (2022). International monetary policy and
     cryptocurrency markets: Dynamic and spillover effects.
     The European Journal of Finance, 30(16), 1855-1875.
     DOI: 10.1080/1351847X.2022.2068375
     URL: https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/full/10.1080/1351847X.2022.2068375
     Note: TVP-VAR: G4 central bank policy rates → BTC/LTC/XRP spillovers;
           extends the Fed-centric frame to international context.

A8.  Buthelezi, E. M. (2025). Cryptocurrency responses to U.S. monetary policy
     shocks: A data-driven exploration of price and volatility patterns.
     The American Economist, 70(1), 94-119.
     DOI: 10.1177/05694345241269036
     URL: https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/10.1177/05694345241269036
     Note: Markov-switching GARCH on 2015-2023 data; tightening decreases
           crypto prices across regimes — similar sample period to yours.

A9.  Choi, S., & Shin, J. (2022). Bitcoin: An inflation hedge but not a safe
     haven. Finance Research Letters, 46(PB).
     DOI: 10.1016/j.frl.2021.102379
     URL: https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S1544612321003810
     Note: VAR separates BTC response to inflation shocks (positive) vs.
           financial uncertainty (negative) — directly covers your VIX and
           dFedRate variables.

A10. Mahmoudi, M. (2023). Examining the effect of monetary policy and monetary
     policy uncertainty on cryptocurrencies market. arXiv:2311.10739.
     URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2311.10739
     *** PREPRINT — not peer-reviewed. Cite as working paper. ***
     Note: MPU decreases BTC returns in both volatility regimes using
           Markov-switching VAR on 2010-2023 data.

A11. Liu, Y., & Tsyvinski, A. (2021). Risks and returns of cryptocurrency.
     The Review of Financial Studies, 34(6), 2689-2727.
     DOI: 10.1093/rfs/hhaa113
     URL: https://academic.oup.com/rfs/article-abstract/34/6/2689/5912024
     Note: Landmark paper: crypto returns had no exposure to standard macro or
           stock-market factors — the foundational baseline your thesis challenges.

CLUSTER B — Bitcoin Halving Effects (7 papers)
================================================================================

B1.  Lashkaripour, M. (2024). Some stylized facts about Bitcoin halving.
     Finance Research Letters, 69(PB).
     DOI: 10.1016/j.frl.2024.106198
     URL: https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S1544612324012273
     Note: Documents that halving sensitivity weakened over successive cycles;
           directly supports H2's prediction of diminished post-ETF effect.

B2.  Fabus, J., Kremenova, I., Stalmasekova, N., & Kvasnicova-Galovicova, T.
     (2024). An empirical examination of Bitcoin's halving effects: Assessing
     cryptocurrency sustainability within the landscape of financial technologies.
     Journal of Risk and Financial Management, 17(6), 229.
     DOI: 10.3390/jrfm17060229
     URL: https://www.mdpi.com/1911-8074/17/6/229
     Note: RSI, MACD, and regression analysis on 2012, 2016, 2020 halvings;
           documents the predictable post-halving price cycle benchmark.

B3.  Veloso, V., Gatsios, R. C., Magnani, V. M., & Lima, F. G. (2025). Is
     Bitcoin's market maturing? Cumulative abnormal returns and volatility in
     the 2024 halving and past cycles.
     Journal of Risk and Financial Management, 18(5), 242.
     DOI: 10.3390/jrfm18050242
     URL: https://www.mdpi.com/1911-8074/18/5/242
     *** VERIFY DOI before submission — very recent publication. ***
     Note: Directly compares all four halvings; finds 2024 showed smaller, less
           significant price reactions — strongest direct support for H2.

B4.  Liu, W., Zhao, X., Li, W., & Wang, Y. (2025). The effect of the
     cryptocurrency halving event. Pacific-Basin Finance Journal, 94, 102913.
     DOI: 10.1016/j.pacfin.2025.102913
     URL: https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0927538X25002501
     *** VERIFY DOI before submission — very recent publication. ***
     Note: Negative CAARs around halving dates; effects attributed to
           attention-driven trading rather than supply fundamentals.

B5.  Subawa, N. S., Mimaki, C. A., Mahendra, I. M. O., & Utami, M. S. M.
     (2025). Bitcoin halving: How effective is it in driving cryptocurrency
     market dynamics? Journal of Risk and Financial Management, 19(1), 2.
     DOI: 10.3390/jrfm19010002
     URL: https://www.mdpi.com/1911-8074/19/1/2
     Note: PLS-SEM: halvings affect prices via sentiment and regulatory channels
           — mechanism explaining why efficiency gains post-ETF reduce the effect.

B6.  Mensi, W., Al-Yahyaee, K. H., & Kang, S. H. (2019). Structural breaks and
     double long memory of cryptocurrency prices: A comparative analysis from
     Bitcoin and Ethereum. Finance Research Letters, 29(C), 222-230.
     DOI: 10.1016/j.frl.2018.07.011
     URL: https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S1544612318303076
     Note: FIGARCH + break dummies applied to BTC — methodological precedent
           for the thesis's structural break approach. Also relevant to Cluster F.

B7.  Liu, Y., Tsyvinski, A., & Wu, X. (2022). Common risk factors in
     cryptocurrency. The Journal of Finance, 77(2), 1133-1177.
     DOI: 10.1111/jofi.13119
     URL: https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/abs/10.1111/jofi.13119
     Note: Identifies crypto-specific momentum and size factors; provides basis
           for comparing pre- vs. post-ETF factor structures (H2).

CLUSTER C — Spot Bitcoin ETF & Institutional Adoption (8 papers)
================================================================================

C1.  Mohamad, A. (2025). Do Bitcoin ETFs lead price discovery following their
     introduction in the Bitcoin market? Computational Economics, 66, 947-969.
     DOI: 10.1007/s10614-025-10998-x
     URL: https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s10614-025-10998-x
     Note: Spot ETFs (IBIT, FBTC, GBTC) dominate price discovery ~85% of the
           time post-Jan 2024 — directly validates your PostETF breakpoint date.

C2.  Kia, K., Song, V. Y., & Xu, K. (2025). Price discovery in Bitcoin ETF
     market. The Financial Review.
     DOI: 10.1111/fire.70026
     URL: https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1111/fire.70026
     Note: After Jan 10, 2024, spot market overtook futures-ETFs as the primary
           price discovery venue — confirms the structural significance of your
           ETF breakpoint.

C3.  Babalos, V., Bouri, E., & Gupta, R. (2025). Does the introduction of US
     spot Bitcoin ETFs affect spot returns and volatility of major
     cryptocurrencies? The Quarterly Review of Economics and Finance, 102.
     DOI: 10.1016/j.qref.2025.102006
     URL: https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S106297692500047X
     Note: ETF introduction increased BTC returns and reduced BTC/XRP volatility
           — direct test of the PostETF dummy effect you model.

C4.  Guliyev, T., & Ahmadova, A. (2025). From flows to value: Cointegration
     between Bitcoin spot ETF assets and Bitcoin price. Ledger, 10, 154-172.
     DOI: 10.5195/ledger.2025.393
     URL: https://www.ledgerjournal.org/ojs/ledger/article/view/393
     Note: ETF AUM cointegrated with BTC prices (Jan 2024 – May 2025);
           confirms institutional flows exert sustained upward price influence.

C5.  Liu, S., & Yang, C. (2024). Spot cryptocurrency ETFs: Crypto investment
     products or stepping stones toward tokenization.
     Finance Research Letters, 69(PB).
     DOI: 10.1016/j.frl.2024.106150
     URL: https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S1544612324011796
     Note: ETF approval elevated BTC legitimacy and liquidity — mechanism-level
           account for why macro factor exposures would change post-ETF (H1).

C6.  Hong, Y., Feng, H., Wang, Y., & Li, B. (2025). The impact of Bitcoin ETF
     approval on Bitcoin's hedging properties against traditional assets.
     arXiv:2512.12815.
     DOI: 10.48550/arXiv.2512.12815
     URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2512.12815
     *** PREPRINT — not peer-reviewed. Cite as working paper. ***
     Note: DCC-GARCH: BTC-equity correlation increased post-ETF, hedging
           effectiveness changed — directly relevant to H1's interaction terms.

C7.  Boan, D., Pham, H., & Nguyen Thanh, B. (2021). Price discovery in the
     cryptocurrency market: Evidence from institutional activity.
     Journal of Industrial and Business Economics, 49(1), 111-131.
     DOI: 10.1007/s40812-021-00202-0
     URL: https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s40812-021-00202-0
     Note: Pre-ETF: institutional Bitcoin futures trading shaped BTC-S&P 500
           correlations — provides the pre-institutional baseline for H1.

C8.  Wu, D. (2025). Institutional adoption and correlation dynamics: Bitcoin's
     evolving role in financial markets. arXiv:2501.09911.
     URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2501.09911
     *** PREPRINT — not peer-reviewed. Cite as working paper. ***
     Note: Rolling BTC-S&P500/Nasdaq correlations peaked at 0.87 in 2024
           following institutional milestones — strong narrative support for H1.

D3.  Corbet, S., Meegan, A., Larkin, C., Lucey, B., & Yarovaya, L. (2018).

     Journal of Risk and Financial Management, 14(7), 282.
     DOI: 10.3390/jrfm14070282
     URL: https://www.mdpi.com/1911-8074/14/7/282

D5.  Hernandez, J. A., Hasan, M. Z., & McIver, R. P. (2023). Bitcoin, gold,
     and the VIX: Short- and long-term effects of economic policy uncertainty.

     DOI: 10.1080/13504851.2021.2018125
     URL: https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/full/10.1080/13504851.2021.2018125
     Note: ARDL: EPU negatively impacts BTC short-term, positively long-term —
           directly pertains to your VIX control variable.

D6.  Yi, E., Yang, B., Jeong, M., et al. (2023). Market efficiency of

     Scientific Reports, 13, 4789.
     DOI: 10.1038/s41598-023-31618-4
     URL: https://www.nature.com/articles/s41598-023-31618-4
     Note: Weak-form efficiency evidence for BTC; context for H2 — more
           efficient markets pre-price halvings, diminishing the effect.

D7.  Marti, R., & Reimat, A. (2025). Bitcoin and global stock portfolio:
     Dynamics across short and long-term horizons.
     Finance Research Letters, 108918.
     DOI: 10.1016/j.frl.2025.108918
     URL: https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S1544612325021713
     Note: 1% MSCI World rise → 4.8% BTC rise by 2025; evidence of deep macro
           integration post-ETF supporting H1's direction.
